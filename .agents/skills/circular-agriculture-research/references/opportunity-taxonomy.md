# Circular Agriculture Opportunity Taxonomy

Use this taxonomy when mapping or comparing opportunities. Select the relevant categories rather than forcing every category into the answer.

## Organic Waste Valorization

- Composting and vermicomposting from crop residues, manure, food waste, market waste, and processing byproducts.
- Outputs: compost, soil amendment blends, potting mixes, farm inputs, service contracts.
- Key risks: contamination, inconsistent feedstock, moisture control, odor, logistics cost, weak buyer trust.

## Nutrient Loops

- Recovery and redistribution of nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, and organic matter from manure, wastewater, digestate, compost, and crop residues.
- Outputs: fertilizer substitutes, soil health programs, nutrient management plans.
- Key risks: nutrient variability, heavy metals or pathogens, regulatory limits, farmer adoption.

## Biochar and Carbon-Rich Soil Amendments

- Conversion of biomass residues into biochar and blended amendments.
- Outputs: soil amendments, water retention products, filtration media, possible carbon-credit projects.
- Key risks: feedstock supply, pyrolysis cost, agronomic proof, MRV complexity, buyer education.

## Anaerobic Digestion and Biogas

- Conversion of manure, food waste, and agro-industrial waste into biogas and digestate.
- Outputs: energy, heat, fertilizer, waste-management service.
- Key risks: capex, maintenance, feedstock consistency, digestate markets, permitting.

## Insect Protein and Biological Conversion

- Black soldier fly or other insect systems that convert organic waste into animal feed ingredients and frass.
- Outputs: protein meal, oil, frass fertilizer, waste processing service.
- Key risks: regulation, feedstock safety, processing standards, customer acceptance, scale economics.

## Crop Residue Utilization

- Use of straw, husk, bagasse, coconut residues, fruit peels, and other residues for materials, feed, energy, mulch, substrates, or soil amendments.
- Outputs: mushroom substrate, animal bedding, packaging, bioenergy, compost inputs.
- Key risks: seasonal availability, collection cost, competing uses, storage, moisture.

## Circular Aquaculture-Agriculture Systems

- Integrated fish, livestock, crop, algae, and water systems that reuse nutrients and reduce waste.
- Outputs: integrated production, water-treatment services, advisory, farm design.
- Key risks: disease, water quality, technical support, monitoring burden.

## Regenerative and Circular Farm Management

- Practices that combine cover crops, compost, reduced tillage, rotations, agroforestry, livestock integration, and input reduction.
- Outputs: advisory services, farm plans, input bundles, certification support.
- Key risks: delayed payoff, measurement difficulty, farmer cash-flow constraints.

## Digital and Platform Opportunities

- Software for waste-stream matching, farm advisory, traceability, logistics, certification, MRV, financing, and cooperative operations.
- Best fit when there is fragmented supply, repeated transactions, evidence requirements, or coordination across many actors.
- Key risks: poor data availability, low willingness to pay, field connectivity, trust, and operational complexity hidden behind the software.
