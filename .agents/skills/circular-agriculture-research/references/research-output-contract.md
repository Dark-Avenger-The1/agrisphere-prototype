# Research Output Contract

Use this structure for formal research briefs and adapt it to the user's depth requirement.

## Compact Brief

1. Executive summary
2. Best opportunities ranked
3. Evidence table: claim, source, year, confidence, caveat
4. Business model implications
5. Risks and unknowns
6. Recommended next steps

## Full Circular Agriculture Report

1. Executive summary
2. Opportunity map table
3. Scientific evidence summary
4. Agribusiness model comparison
5. Policy and funding landscape
6. Case studies and named actors
7. Risks and implementation barriers
8. Recommended first pilots or experiments
9. Source list grouped by academic, government/NGO, industry, and news

## Questions To Answer

- Which circular models have the strongest evidence of economic viability?
- Which reduce waste, emissions, fertilizer cost, input dependency, or logistics burden?
- What business models exist: input production, waste collection, processing, advisory, carbon credits, cooperative models, B2B supply, or integrated farm systems?
- What policies, grants, standards, or incentives support adoption?
- Who are the leading companies, NGOs, universities, government actors, and buyers?
- What barriers matter most: capex, logistics, farmer adoption, regulation, quality control, contamination, market access, or unit economics?
- What is the strongest opportunity for a small or mid-sized entrant?
