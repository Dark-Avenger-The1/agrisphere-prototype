# Circular Agriculture and Agribusiness Research Prompt

```text
Research circular agriculture and agribusiness opportunities for [audience], with emphasis on [country/region].

Goal:
- Identify evidence-backed circular agriculture models that could become practical agribusiness opportunities.
- Separate scientific evidence, policy support, market opportunity, and operational constraints.

Scope:
- Geography: [Philippines / Southeast Asia / global benchmark]
- Time period: prioritize 2020-present, but include older foundational studies when important.
- Include: nutrient recycling, composting, biochar, anaerobic digestion, insect protein, food waste valorization, crop residue utilization, manure management, regenerative agriculture, circular aquaculture-agriculture systems, and farm-to-market waste loops.
- Exclude: purely ideological sustainability claims without data.

Questions to answer:
1. What circular agriculture models have the strongest evidence of economic viability?
2. Which models reduce waste, emissions, fertilizer costs, or input dependency?
3. What business models exist: input production, waste collection, processing, advisory, carbon credits, cooperative models, B2B supply, or integrated farm systems?
4. What policies, grants, standards, or incentives support adoption?
5. Who are the leading companies, NGOs, universities, or government actors?
6. What barriers matter most: capex, logistics, farmer adoption, regulation, quality control, contamination, market access, or unit economics?
7. What are the best opportunities for a small or mid-sized agribusiness entrant?

Data to extract:
- Market size or proxy indicators
- Costs and revenue drivers
- Yield, soil, emissions, or waste reduction metrics
- Named case studies
- Countries and dates
- Source quality and confidence

Output:
1. Executive summary
2. Opportunity map table
3. Scientific evidence summary
4. Agribusiness model comparison
5. Policy and funding landscape
6. Case studies
7. Risks and implementation barriers
8. Recommended first experiments or pilots
9. Source list grouped into academic, government/NGO, industry, and news
```
