# Source Quality Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate Deep Research results before trusting the output.

## Strong Sources

- Peer-reviewed journal articles
- Systematic reviews and meta-analyses
- Government statistics and policy documents
- Intergovernmental sources such as FAO, World Bank, OECD, UNEP, IFAD, and CGIAR
- University extension publications and research institutes
- Company filings, official reports, and audited disclosures

## Medium Sources

- Industry reports with transparent methodology
- NGO reports with cited data
- Reputable trade publications
- Conference papers or working papers from credible institutions

## Weak Sources

- Unsourced blog posts
- Vendor marketing pages
- News articles repeating claims without primary data
- Paywalled snippets without enough context
- Old sources used for current market claims

## Required Checks

- Are citations attached to the most important claims?
- Are dates visible for time-sensitive claims?
- Are estimates labeled as estimates?
- Are academic and market sources separated?
- Are conflicts between sources called out?
- Are limitations and missing evidence explicit?
